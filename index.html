About me

I am an M.Eng. student in CSAIL at MIT, working in the Distributed Robotics Laboratory under the supervision of Prof. Daniela Rus. My primary research interest is in robotic perception and sensing, specifically in the problems of semantic scene understanding and world representation - how we can extract meaningful information about the world given unstructured sensor data. I am also generally interested in the areas of Bayesian modeling and inference, machine learning, and robotic localization and mapping.

My current research focuses on developing algorithms for the detection of unpaved roads in rural environments. Through my research, I hope to develop perception and semantic scene understanding algorithms that enable autonomous mobile vehicles to have a better understanding of their environment.

Previously, I was a SuperUROP in the Spoken Language Systems group at MIT under the supervision of Dr. Jim Glass, and a UROP in the Aerospace Controls Laboratory under the supervision of Dr. Golnaz Habibi and Prof. Jonathan How.
